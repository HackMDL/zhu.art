About me

Alvina Zhurat

My name is Alvina! I've been painting my whole life! I want to tell you how, in principle, it all started! I will not embellish and say that I always knew that art was my vocation, to be honest, I never made any serious plans about painting. I painted only for myself!

I entered the Academy of Arts without having any goals and plans ... Honestly, I have no idea how I decided to go to study in a foreign country, where I don't know anyone at all, I was very much afraid, everyone discouraged me, but I felt that I was on the right track! It was in the Academy that I realized who I am and who I want to become! Everything changed overnight! It was an exhibition-competition at the embassy, where I was a participant!

So where did it all start? No, not from the competition and not even from that picture, to be honest, that evening I did not win anything, nothing special happened then. But still, after that evening, something has changed! For the first time I put my painting on public display!

And you know what? I like it! I liked the atmosphere, I liked the people. I realized that I want to live such a life!

After that evening, I started telling my friends that I wanted to become a real artist, but they looked at me in bewilderment and did not understand what I was talking about, because I had been drawing all my life and I already had 5 years of painting training behind me.

But for me there is a big difference between a person who just paints and an artist. An artist is not a profession or a hobby. An artist is a way of life!

Since then I have completely devoted my life to art!

Artist statment

The ability to create and declare oneself as an artist is what I live with now! I love that I can bring beauty and harmony to this world!

In my works, there is an interest in the human body, it is body language that helps me create emotion in my paintings!

I carry the idea of loving myself and the world around us.

My works do not tell a story, the plots depicted on them should only evoke emotion: warmth, comfort and harmony.

I am often asked: - "What is your picture about? What did you want to show in it?" - and I always answer that what is important is not what I wanted to say (show), much more important is what you saw in it! Each person sees the world around through the prism of his own self. Two people looking at the same object can see completely different things. Most often, looking at a picture, you do not see the author in it, you see a reflection of yourself in it!
